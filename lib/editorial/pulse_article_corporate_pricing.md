# Industry Insights | The Business of Being a Queen: Pricing for Corporate Seekers

## Beyond the Bar: Navigating the Corporate Drag Economy

For many performers, the "Club Rate" is the primary financial metric. It’s a flat fee, usually negotiated via DM or a quick chat with a promoter, often covering a couple of numbers and a host spot. But as the global demand for drag expands into brand activations, luxury hotel residencies, and corporate galas, the "Club Rate" no longer applies.

Transitioning to the corporate market requires more than just a sharper wing—it requires a fundamental shift in how you value your labor. To thrive in this high-stakes environment, you need **Professional Pricing and Invoicing.**

---

## 1. The Value Shift: What are you actually selling?

Corporate seekers (Event Planners, HR Managers, CMOs) aren't just buying a performance; they are buying an experience, a brand alignment, and—most importantly—peace of mind.

When pricing for a corporate client, you must account for:
*   **Preparation & Customization:** Are you creating a custom look for a brand launch?
*   **Time Commitment:** Unlike a 10-minute club set, corporate gigs often involve hours of "Meet & Greet" or atmospheric presence.
*   **Exclusivity & Usage:** Is the brand using your image for their social media ads?
*   **Liability & Professionalism:** They expect a performer who arrives on time, has a technical rider ready, and can provide a formal invoice.
