## 1. What is Public Liability Insurance (PLI)?

At its core, PLI protects you (the performer) against the financial consequences of accidental injury to a member of the public or damage to property during your act. 

Imagine a glass breaks during a high-energy dance number, or a piece of your costume causes a trip hazard for a guest. Without insurance, you could be personally liable for thousands of pounds in damages. PLI ensures that you, the seeker, and the venue are all protected.

**The UK Standard:** Most professional venues and corporate planners require a minimum of **£5 Million** in cover, with many high-end ballrooms and festivals requesting **£10 Million**.

## 2. Why UK Venues Demand It

For hospitality groups and event producers, safety and compliance are top priorities. Large groups like **Stonegate**, **Merivale**, and **The Grand** operate under strict health and safety protocols. 

*   **Risk Mitigation:** Insured performers are lower-risk for the venue's legal department.
*   **Contractual Compliance:** PLI is often a mandatory clause in B2B booking contracts.
*   **Professional Trust:** Providing a current PLI certificate instantly signals that you understand the business side of show business.

## 4. How to Get Covered

If you don't already have PLI, there are several industry-standard options in the UK:
*   **Equity:** Membership often includes PLI as a benefit.
