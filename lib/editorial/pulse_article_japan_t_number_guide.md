## The New Standard: Navigating Japan’s Qualified Invoice System

For performers operating in the Japanese market, the landscape of business compliance changed significantly in late 2023 with the introduction of the **Qualified Invoice System**. 

As DRAGDEM expands into Tokyo’s vibrant scene—from the epicenter of Shinjuku Ni-chome to the high-end corporate events of Roppongi—understanding the "T-Number" has become essential for any artist looking to secure high-value B2B bookings. ---

## 1. What is a T-Number?

A **T-Number** (Registered Number) is a 13-digit identifier issued by the National Tax Agency of Japan. It signifies that a business (including freelance performers) is a "Registered Invoice Issuer."

**Why it matters:** Under the new system, corporate seekers can only claim consumption tax (JCT) credits if the invoice they receive includes a valid T-Number. Without it, the booking becomes more expensive for the seeker, making you less competitive in the B2B market.

## 3. How to Obtain a T-Number

If you are a performer based in Japan (or with a Japanese business entity), you can apply for a T-Number through your local tax office or via the e-Tax portal. While it requires registering for consumption tax, the ability to secure higher-paying corporate gigs often outweighs the administrative effort.

**なぜ重要なのか：** 新しい制度の下では、企業側のクライアントは、受け取った請求書に有効なTナンバーが記載されている場合にのみ、消費税の仕入税額控除を受けることができます。Tナンバーがない場合、クライアントにとっての実質的なコストが増加するため、B2B市場においてあなたの競争力が低下することになります。
